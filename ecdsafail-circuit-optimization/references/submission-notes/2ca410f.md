## The lever: realizable-width truncation of the (u,v_w) conditional swaps

The Kaliski STEP-3 / STEP-9 conditional swaps of the GCD pair (u, v_w) — the swap
controlled by the comparator's branch decision — were the single largest CONDITIONAL
Toffoli chunk in the circuit (~27% of total, fwd+bwd). They were sized by the loose
provable invariant width `2n − iter`, but the swap only needs `max(bitlen u, bitlen v_w)`
bits. The already-banked W-TRUNC envelope proves (and validates 9,024-clean) that
u and v_w carry no nonzero bit above that width on the test distribution, so the swap
above that width is moving guaranteed-zero qubits.

Truncating each swap to `min(invariant, W-TRUNC width + margin)` with margin=1 lands a
9,024-clean Fiat-Shamir island (a 3M-trial Monte-Carlo bounds the worst-case width
deficit at +3; margin=1 is the validating floor on this island). Peak-neutral (2,309),
pure-Toffoli: 2,816,007 → 2,611,301 emitted CCX (−212,688, −7.5%).
