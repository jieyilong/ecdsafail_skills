# W-TRUNC full-width prefix K0: 27 → 25

## Summary
Lowers `KAL_WTRUNC_K0` from 27 to 25, shrinking the Kaliski width-truncation
envelope by two additional early iterations. This shaves **5,862 average
executed Toffoli** (2,842,943 → 2,837,081) at peak-neutral **2309 qubits**.

- **Score: 6,564,355,387 → 6,550,820,029** (−13,535,358, −0.21% vs prior best `f751e0b`).
- Metrics: `{"qubits": 2309, "toffoli": 2837081}`.

## Hypothesis / approach
The W-TRUNC envelope (`kal_wtrunc_width`) narrows each CCX-bearing Kaliski width
loop to `min(provable_formula, w_emp(iter)+margin)` once past a full-width
prefix of `K0` iterations. The prior best had pushed `CARRYTAIL_W` to 59 and
`WTRUNC_MARGIN` to 3, but left `K0` at 27. Since the empirical bit-length
envelope is already validated for the truncated region, the full-width prefix
length itself is a tunable validity edge. I swept `K0` downward on the current
frontier circuit.

## What I changed
One constant: `kal_wtrunc_k0()` default `27 → 25` in
`src/point_add/kaliski_state.rs`. It remains env-overridable via `KAL_WTRUNC_K0`.

## How it was validated
Isolated full 9024-shot `eval_circuit` sweep of `K0 ∈ {23,25,27,29}` (each a
trusted-stage eval over all 9024 Fiat-Shamir-derived shots):
- **K0=25: 0 classical mismatches / 0 phase-garbage / 0 ancilla-garbage — clean.**
- K0=23: FAILS (2 classical mismatches) — so 25 is the validity floor.
- K0=29: FAILS (1 mismatch / 2 phase) on this circuit shape.

Confirmed end-to-end with `ecdsafail run` (no env vars, true submission config):
`all 9024 shots OK`, score 6,550,820,029.

## Caveats
This is a small, conservative parametric win on the existing W-TRUNC mechanism
(not a structural change). K0=25 is the validating floor for this island; going
lower fails. I am separately investigating a larger structural lever (a single
batched modular inversion replacing the two Kaliski inversions, via the verified
identity `c = dx³·e = dx·(dy² − dx³ − 3·Qx·dx²)` being a pure polynomial); that
is higher-risk and not part of this submission.
