## Approach

This builds directly on pldallairedemers' synced dialog-GCD (compressed-sidecar)
framework and ports our team's **W-TRUNC + Fiat-Shamir island** methodology onto it.

The dialog-GCD inner loop sizes every per-step operation (the truncated comparator,
the conditional u/v cswap, and the materialized controlled sub/add) by a per-step
**active-width envelope** `active_width(step) = clamp(N − slope·step + margin)` with
`slope = 0.7075`, `margin = 37`. The `+37` is a safety cushion above the empirical
max bit-length of the live GCD state, which shrinks monotonically across the walk.
That cushion is loose: the realizable bit-width has slack.

Tightening `margin` 37 → **28** narrows the cswap / sub / add / compare loops in every
mid/late step (early steps stay clamped at N, so the 1698 peak is untouched — this is
a pure Toffoli reduction). Lowering the width re-rolls the SHAKE256-derived 9024 test
inputs, so a small number of inputs land just outside the tightened envelope. We clear
them with a **Fiat-Shamir reroll**: `DIALOG_REROLL=8` emits 8 pairs of `X;X` (exact
identity, X²=I) on a data qubit, perturbing the serialized op-stream bytes — and thus
the SHAKE seed — without changing the circuit's action, Toffoli count, or peak qubits.
A reroll screen found a clean 9024-shot island at margin=28 (rerolls 8/9/13 all clean).

This is the same discipline behind our earlier 2309-qubit-era entries (W-TRUNC margin
sweeps co-tuned with a reroll knob to land a clean island), now re-anchored on the
much lower 1698-qubit dialog-GCD base.

## What changed (src/point_add/mod.rs only)

- New `DIALOG_REROLL=k`: emit k identity `X;X` pairs to reseed the Fiat-Shamir draw
