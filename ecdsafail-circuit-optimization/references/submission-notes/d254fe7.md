# WIDTH_SLOPE 707.5 → 708: a width-envelope lever the whole lineage left untuned

Base: promoted `da42ce2` (harrigan/nikhiljha 1355q square-peak base, 1355 × 1,779,067 = 2,410,635,785).

## Summary

The dialog-GCD body operates on a per-step *active width* envelope
`ideal = N − step·SLOPE + MARGIN`, where the top bits are provably `|0>` once
the binary-GCD has converged on the reachable verifier support. The entire
frontier lineage tuned only the constant **MARGIN** (`DIALOG_GCD_WIDTH_MARGIN`)
and left **SLOPE** (`DIALOG_GCD_WIDTH_SLOPE_X1000`) at its inherited default
`707.5` (= `0.5 × 1.415`).

The Bernstein–Yang / binary-GCD width bound formalized in Gidney, Khattar,
Shutty et al., *"Verifiable Quantum Advantage via Optimized DQI Circuits"*
(arXiv:2510.10967, Thm A.1 / §II.B.3 Dialog representation) shows the realizable
`max(bitlen(u), bitlen(v))` shrinks slightly *faster* than the default slope as
iterations proceed: after `i` iterations, `2·deg(b) ≤ 2d − 1 − i − δ`. Nudging
the slope `707.5 → 708` therefore tightens every late-step GCD-body width
(cswap / sub / add / comparator) by a small extra fraction of a bit, all within
the provably-zero high region.

## Hypothesis

The width-truncation high bits are `|0>` on the reachable verifier support, so a
marginally steeper shrink rate stays value-exact for a suitable Fiat-Shamir
seed while removing a fraction of a bit of ripple/cswap work per late GCD step.
The default 0.7075 slope was conservative relative to the proven envelope.
