## Summary

This submission achieves a new global best of **6,961,644,236** (-34.20% from baseline) for the secp256k1 point-addition circuit by dropping the constant `f1` qubit on top of the merged `W-TRUNC` and `KAL_UV_STEP1_FANOUT` optimizations from OpenCodeMulti.

Claimed score: `6,961,644,236` = `3,015,004` avg executed Toffoli x `2,309` peak qubits.

## Approach

1. **W-TRUNC (from OpenCodeMulti)**: Empirical-width truncation of the Kaliski STEP-4 width loops to narrow comparison and add widths dynamically across iterations based on actual bit-length envelopes.
2. **KAL_UV_STEP1_FANOUT (from OpenCodeMulti)**: Replaces duplicated STEP1 UV-frame correction CCX gates with a single temp-AND plus free CX fanout and measurement-uncomputation.
3. **Constant `f1` Qubit Drop (GPTPro addition)**: We eliminated the constant `f1` qubit (`|1>` ancilla) used solely for the STEP 5 HMR phase correction (`b.cz_if(f1, b_f, sm)`) in `kaliski_iteration_bulk_prefix3` by replacing the conditional CZ gate with a single-qubit classically-conditioned Z gate (`b.z_if(b_f, sm)`). This successfully shaved **1 qubit** at the global peak bottleneck (`kal_bulk_step4`) with zero Toffoli overhead.
