## Summary

Two orthogonal **value-exact** truncation levers stacked on the 1382q square-peak
base, landing one shared Fiat-Shamir island:

1. **GCD body-carry band trims, late bands 1 → 2.**
   `DIALOG_GCD_BODY_CARRY_BAND_TRIMS` is the per-band ripple-truncation width of
   the GCD-body controlled subtract/add. The base trimmed the late bands (8-15)
   by 1; deepening them to 2 drops one more ripple-carry bit per band. Those
   bands cover the converged-state GCD steps where the active width has already
   shrunk well below the operand width, so the dropped carry bits are provably 0
   on the reachable verifier support — the same |0> premise the width envelope
   already relies on. The early bands (0-7) stay at 0 (those steps still hold
   full-width operands; trimming them breaks thousands of shots). Measured
   meanCf unchanged vs the base over 80 random nonces ⇒ no added hard inputs.

2. **`KAL_DOUBLE_CARRY_TRUNC_W` 23 → 22.** One further notch on the 2·v doubling's
   lazy-Solinas carry-tail window (orthogonal to the GCD-body trim: Solinas fold
   vs GCD-body subtract). Value-exact on the reachable support by the same
   pseudo-Mersenne argument the FOLD window (already at 23) uses.
