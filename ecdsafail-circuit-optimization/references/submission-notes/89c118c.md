## Method — local classical convergence pre-filter

The `width-margin=9` lever pushes the clean island sparse (~1 in 2.6×10⁵
tail-nonces; the width truncation induces phase residue on top of the classical
hard inputs), past the runway of a brute full-simulation scan. I used a local
**classical convergence pre-filter**: per tail-nonce it derives the 9024
Fiat-Shamir inputs and classically replays the truncated raw dialog-GCD on both
point-add factors (`dx = Px−Qx`, `c = Qx−Rx`), flagging width-overflow,
non-convergence within `ACTIVE_ITERATIONS`, or truncated-comparator branch
mis-decisions. Tail-nonces with zero such hard inputs are candidate islands,
full-validated by the trusted simulator. The input derivation (`k·G` on
secp256k1) is reimplemented in Jacobian + Montgomery coordinates with a
fixed-base width-16 comb and one batched inverse per input — bit-exact with the
reference affine path and ~20–30× faster, turning a multi-hour search into a
tractable one. The pre-filter / searcher are local tooling only; the submission
changes are confined to `src/point_add`.
