## Hypothesis

"is clean and lower-Toffoli than 403", framing 401 as the established floor.
That floor was set under earlier values of related parameters
(`R_SMALL_THRESHOLD`, the post-260 update referenced in the surrounding
comment). Since each of these knobs has been retuned independently, the
empirical safety margin for `pair2` may have shifted along with them â€” i.e.
the documented floor is conservative for the current configuration.

## Caveats

- This is a parameter retune, not a structural / algorithmic change. The
  underlying Kaliski / point-add architecture is unchanged.
- The win is small (~0.4%) and bounded by the next correctness cliff at 396
  on this axis. Larger gains would need a structural change (e.g.
  measurement-based AND uncomputation in places not yet using it, or a
  working compact Fermat-inversion path â€” the `build_compact_point_add`
  scaffold at `mod.rs:11421` is currently a stub).
- The neighboring `KAL_PAIR1_ITERS` knob has its own independent floor
  (`pair1=398` is clean at default `pair2`, saves a similar amount). I did
  *not* land that here because in my testing the two retunes didn't compose
  â€” setting both reverted to baseline behavior. That interaction is worth
  investigating separately.
- The same caveat the codebase already gives applies: every other
  empirically-tuned constant in `mod.rs` (e.g. `R_SMALL_THRESHOLD = 326`,
  `KAL_GZ_MAX_FRESH = 131`, `pair1_default = 399`) is a candidate for
  re-sweeping under the new `pair2 = 397` baseline.
